# Market Description. The Iridium ADC is enabled by the development of several integrated circuits. Balanced inputs afford better environmental noise immunity, while the option for single ended input allows versatility of measurement setup. The low noise front end is enabled by the fully differential operational amplifiers produced by Texas Instruments. The OPA1632 fully differential amplifier boasts incredibly low distortion and noise specifications at 0.000022% and 1.7 nV/sqrt(Hz) respectively. It is performance like this that enable the use of 24 bit converters. This feeds into the impressive Analog Devices AD7760 delta-sigma ADC capable of 24 bit conversion with a maximum output data rate of 2.5 MSPS for a bandwidth of 1.25 kHz. This ADC features adjustable output data rates allowing a selection that suits the particular test. It also features non aliasing FIR low pass filter simplifying the front end analog design. We also get guaranteed monotonic conversion across all 24 bits, with the addition of excellent linearity of conversion that comes along with delta-sigma converters. A calibrated voltage reference ensures that converted data is based on a known standard. All of this is controlled by a microcontroller that converts the data to USB Audio class PCM and powered by a low noise, linear power supply for clean, quiet, smooth power. Presently, the devices produced by Audio Precision are best-in-class devices. But only their most expensive model has a bandwidth exceeding 90 kHz, and their cheapest unit cost several thousand dollars. Many consumer audio interfaces offer 24 or even 32 bit converters, and can offer sample rates of up to 192 kHz while maintaining distortion figures down to just a few parts per million. This is similar to Audio Precision, and they do this for just a few hundred dollars. Their usefulness for is limited for because they do not offer a stable or known reference voltage. They must be calibrated with each use and the reference tends to drift heavily with temperature and age.
